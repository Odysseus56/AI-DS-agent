**Difference-in-Differences Analysis Explained**

Difference-in-differences (DiD) is a statistical method used to estimate the effect of a specific intervention or treatment when you have data from two groups over time: one group that is affected by the intervention (the treatment group) and another that is not (the control group).

**How It Works:**
1. **Two Groups**: You compare two groups - one that experiences a change (like a new marketing strategy) and one that does not.
2. **Before and After**: You look at the data before and after the intervention for both groups.
3. **Calculate Differences**: You calculate the difference in outcomes (like sales or customer engagement) for both groups over time.
4. **Subtract the Differences**: The key is to subtract the change in the control group from the change in the treatment group. This helps isolate the effect of the intervention from other factors that might influence the outcome.
